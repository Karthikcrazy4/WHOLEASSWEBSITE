Federated Learning Explained

  • Collaborative training of a model across distributed data sources without sharing sensitive data.
  • Participants train a pre-trained model locally and send encrypted updates to a central server.
  • The server aggregates updates iteratively to refine the model.
  • Types of Federated Learning:
    • Horizontal: Similar datasets used for training.
    • Vertical: Complementary datasets combined (e.g., movie and book reviews).
    • Transfer: Pre-trained model adapted for a new task.
  • Applications include fraud detection, healthcare diagnostics, and personalized services.

Benefits of Breaking Down Data Silos

  • Enables data utilization without compromising privacy or compliance.
  • Applications include:
    • Health care: Aggregated medical data for disease detection.
    • Banking: Enhancing credit scoring and fraud detection.
    • Insurance: Road and driver safety improvements using pooled claims data.
    • Manufacturing: Detecting defects and machine breakdowns.
    • Edge devices: Data from sensors for climate predictions.
  • Preserves privacy while unlocking data potential.

Balancing the Privacy-Accuracy Trade-off

  • Exchanging model updates risks exposing data to inference attacks.
  • Privacy solutions include:
    • Secure multi-party computation: Encrypts updates.
    • Differential privacy: Adds noise to data.
  • Application-specific trade-offs between privacy and accuracy.

Challenges: Efficiency, Transparency, and Incentives

  • Efficiency: High bandwidth and computational demands.
    • Solutions: Model pruning and compression.
  • Transparency: Ensuring accuracy and fairness without accessing private data.
    • Solutions: Encryption frameworks like DeTrust for auditing and consensus.
  • Data control: Retraining models when hosts leave. Solutions are emerging to minimize full retraining.
  • Trust and incentives: Addressing phony data contributions with incentives for truthful participation.
